Questions & Answers

Frequently Asked

Please confirm your attendance by February 1st, 2027. This allows us to finalise catering, seating, and arrangements for your comfort. Late RSVPs may not be accommodated.

Due to venue capacity, attendance is limited to those named on the invitation. If your invitation includes a guest, their name will appear on it. Kindly reach out to us directly if you have any questions.

Children of immediate family are warmly welcome. We kindly ask that other guests arrange childcare so that all adults can enjoy the evening fully. Please indicate the number of children attending in your RSVP.

Nigerian traditional attire is warmly encouraged — Aso-Ebi, Ankara, Isiagu, Agbada, and Kaftan are all welcome. Our colour palette is Olive, Gold, Burgundy, Ebony and Cream. Western formal wear is also acceptable. Please avoid white, which is reserved for the bride.

Yes, secure parking will be available at and around the venue. Attendants will be on hand to direct you. We also recommend using Bolt or Uber to avoid parking altogether, especially for the evening reception.

We have a professional photography and videography team capturing every moment. During the ceremony itself, we kindly ask guests to keep phones away so everyone can be fully present. After the ceremony, you are free to take as many photos as you like and share them using our wedding hashtag.

Absolutely. The feast will feature a generous spread of traditional Igbo dishes alongside options for vegetarian, vegan, and gluten-free guests. Please indicate your meal preference when completing your RSVP so we can ensure you are well catered for.

Guests are invited to arrive from 12:00 PM. The first ceremony events begin at 1:10 PM. Nigerian time is beautiful — but for this occasion, please do be punctual.
